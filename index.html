How it works
A day with Paw Track

Here's what it actually looks like to use the app. No setup guide, no 20-minute tutorial. Just your morning, your pet, and one tap at a time.

🌅
7:00 AM
Morning notification
Your lock screen says "Good morning! Luna had 3 meals and 2 walks yesterday." Tap it and you land right on the home screen, ready to start logging.
🍽️
7:15 AM
Log breakfast
One tap on the meal button. Done. Your daily goal ring fills up a little. You're 1 of 4 tasks done for the day.
🐕
8:30 AM
Morning walk
Hit start, put your phone in your pocket. The GPS tracks your route, distance, steps, and duration. When you're done, the walk shows up on your home feed with a map.
🏆
8:45 AM
Badge unlocked
You just hit a 7-day streak. A celebration overlay pops up: "One week. Every single day." Below the dismiss button, you see your next badge is 63% done. You'll come back tomorrow.
💊
6:00 PM
Medication reminder
Your phone buzzes: "💊 Medication · Luna. Time for her evening dose. Tap to log." You tap, it opens to the right screen, and the card pulses so you know exactly where to tap.
📸
9:00 PM
Share your streak
Your 7-day streak card is on the home feed. Tap share, and it generates an Instagram Story card with fire particles and "One week. Every single day." in your voice, not the app's.

Under the hood
Why you'll actually keep using it

Most pet apps get deleted within a week. Paw Track is designed around behavioral patterns that make daily care feel like something you look forward to.

Streaks

Loss aversion keeps you going

Once you've built a 14-day streak, the thought of breaking it feels worse than the effort of logging. That's by design. Seven milestone levels from 3 days to a full year.

Badges

Progress you can almost see

The home feed shows your next closest badge with a progress bar. If you're 63% of the way to Walk Legend, you'll want to finish it. That's the Zeigarnik Effect: incomplete things nag at you.

Celebration

Every milestone feels personal

Badge celebrations don't just say "Early Bird unlocked." They say "Up early for Luna. That's love." Thirty-nine unique emotional subtitles, one for every badge, personalized with your pet's name.

Sharing

Social proof that fits your feed

Share cards are designed to look like something you'd post, not an ad. Streak cards get fire particles. Birthday cards use your pet's photo as the background. Walk routes look like Strava.

Notifications

Nudges, not guilt trips

Morning messages reference what you did yesterday. Evening messages review the day. Day 1 users get encouragement. Day 30 users get momentum. The tone adapts to where you are in your journey.

Onboarding

Guided, not overwhelming

Animated spotlights walk you through each feature one at a time. Complete three key actions and you get a mini celebration. It teaches by doing, not by reading a help page.
